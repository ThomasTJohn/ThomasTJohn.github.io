Thomas T. John, Ph.D.

Assistant Teaching Professor of Mathematics  |  Syracuse University  |  Statistical Consultant

Statistical Consulting

My consulting practice spans two decades of applied statistical work across industry, healthcare, and academia. Engagements range from single-deliverable analyses to multi-year embedded partnerships — from Fortune 500 market research firms to medical device companies to university research teams. The common thread is a commitment to getting the methodology right and delivering something that actually works in the hands of the client.

Industry & Market Research

My industry work is rooted in a decade at KS&R (Knowledge Systems & Research), where I served as the firm’s sole statistician, followed by continued independent practice. Engagements have spanned telecommunications, technology, logistics, automotive, manufacturing, and financial services.

  • Discrete Choice & Conjoint Analysis: Full-cycle execution of CBC and MaxDiff studies — experimental design, survey logistics, Hierarchical Bayes estimation via Sawtooth, and simulator development for client-facing scenario modeling.
  • Market Segmentation & Classification: End-to-end segmentation studies including basis variable selection, clustering, segment profiling, and deployment — including multilingual Excel-based classification tools for international salesforce use.
  • Reporting Automation: Design and implementation of automated analytical pipelines — including a VBA-based system that replaced a multi-person manual process for SPSS-to-PowerPoint delivery.
  • Survey Design & Weighting: Question design, stratified sampling, and post-hoc weighting for complex survey programs across B2B and B2C contexts.
  • Customer Equity Modeling: Structural equation modeling and canonical correlation applied to customer lifetime value frameworks across Asia-Pacific markets.
Clinical & Biostatistics

Clinical consulting work has centered on medical device validation, biostatistical analysis for peer-reviewed publication, and statistical methods training for clinical research professionals.

  • Medical Device Validation: Biostatistical analysis supporting device accuracy studies, including Bland-Altman plots, regression analysis, and summary statistics for use in product documentation and published research.
  • Peer-Reviewed Publication: Statistical contributions to clinical research appearing in Blood Pressure Monitoring, Journal of Nursing Administration, and Advances in Human Factors and Ergonomics in Healthcare (Springer).
  • Clinical Researcher Training: Invited presentations to the Society of Clinical Research Associates (NY Chapter) covering statistical methods for clinical researchers, 2013–2023.
Work still in active use

Statistical analysis and Bland-Altman methodology developed for Welch Allyn’s blood pressure device validation program is embedded in product documentation currently hosted by Hillrom (now a Baxter company) and used in ongoing commercial and clinical contexts. The underlying peer-reviewed research — Alpert et al., Blood Pressure Monitoring, 2019 — remains publicly available via DOI. The Hillrom clinical research summary is available here.

Additional Engagements
  • R/Shiny Development: Web portal and Excel simulator development with credential-based access management for restricted team use.
  • Higher Education Research: Cohort persistence analysis for a university College of Engineering, surfacing actionable patterns through cross-tabulation and data slicing.
  • Academic & Cross-Disciplinary Consulting: Statistical support for graduate research, dissertation committees, and faculty across disciplines.
How I work: I come in, understand the problem quickly, and build what’s needed — whether that’s a one-time analysis, a reusable pipeline, or a presentation-ready deliverable. I’m comfortable in unfamiliar environments and under deadline. Clients across market research, healthcare, and academia have called on me precisely when the stakes were high and the timeline was short. I leave you with something that works — and that you understand.
